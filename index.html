Methodology

This visualization was created as a sample project to highlight relevant skills. The process aimed for scalability for potential future work on mapping chemical facilities in China.

  1. Data Sourcing & Challenges: I began by researching lists of chemical plants in Shandong, aiming to find facilities that were classified as "key energy consumption units". However, I was unable to access most of the relevant Shandong government websites, potentially due to foreign IP address restrictions. I found potential commercial datasets, but did not purchase them due to budget constraints for this sample project. A publicly available list of ~1500 Shandong chemical companies (including addresses and registered capital) was used as the primary data source, with registered capital serving as a proxy for operational size.
  2. Data Scoping: To manage processing time within technical constraints, I narrowed the list to the largest 200 companies by registered capital for geocoding and name translation using free tools.
  3. Data Processing & Enhancement: I made Python scripts to scrape data from the primary source website, and then processed, cleaned, and enriched the data. Google Geocoding API was used to obtain latitude/longitude coordinates from Chinese addresses. The Google Translate API was used to translate company names into English. The processed data was exported to a CSV file, which served as the data source for the Flourish map.
  4. Map Development: I chose to use Flourish to create the map due to its map customization options and ease of use. I adapted a map of China to focus specifically on Shandong Province. Lastly, I uploaded the CSV data to Flourish to plot company.
  5. Documentation (This Page): This webpage serves as documentation, outlining the process, tools, challenges, and potential refinements to ensure the methodology is understandable and potentially scalable for future work by colleagues.

Potential Refinements & Next Steps

With adequate time, budget, and resources, the following improvements could significantly enhance the dataset and visualization:

  • Add Main Products Data: Incorporating main products for each facility is crucial for understanding process types and potential energy intensity of each facility. This can be obtained from commercial datasets.
  • Improve Geocoding Accuracy: Use Baidu Maps Geocoding API or other China-specific mapping services, as they generally offer higher accuracy for Chinese addresses compared to Google Maps API.
  • Access Government Data: Use a Chinese proxy server or VPN to avoid IP restrictions and access official government databases for more comprehensive and verified facility lists.
  • Acquire Commercial Datasets: Research and potentially purchase commercial datasets focused on the Chinese chemical industry (identified options were under $100), which offer more detailed information than publicly available sources.
