Methodology

This visualization was created as a sample project to demonstrate relevant skills. The process aimed for scalability for potential future work on mapping chemical facilities in China.

  1. Data Sourcing & Challenges: Initial research sought lists of chemical plants in Shandong, particularly focusing on energy consumption data (>10k tce). Accessing official Shandong government websites proved difficult, potentially due to IP address restrictions. Commercial datasets were identified but not purchased due to budget constraints for this sample project. A publicly available list of ~1500 Shandong chemical companies (including addresses and registered capital) was used as the primary data source, with registered capital serving as an imperfect proxy for operational size.
  2. Data Scoping: To manage processing time within the constraints, the list was narrowed down to a sample of 200 companies for location identification and name translation using free tools.
  3. Data Processing & Enhancement: A GitHub Codespace environment was used with Python scripts for data scraping (where applicable), processing, cleaning, and enhancement. Google Geocoding API was utilized to obtain latitude/longitude coordinates from Chinese addresses. Google Translate API was used to translate company names into English. The processed data was exported to a CSV file.
  4. Map Development: The Flourish platform was chosen for map creation due to its efficient handling of CSV data and map customization options. A map of China was adapted to focus specifically on Shandong Province. The processed CSV data was imported to plot the company information.
  5. Documentation (This Page): This HTML page serves as documentation, outlining the process, tools, challenges, and potential refinements to ensure the methodology is understandable and potentially scalable for future work by colleagues.

Potential Refinements & Next Steps

Given adequate time, budget, and resources (as would be available in an employment context), the following improvements could significantly enhance the dataset and visualization:

  • Add Main Products Data: Incorporating main products for each facility is crucial for understanding process types and potential energy intensity. This can be obtained from commercial datasets.
  • Improve Geocoding Accuracy: Utilize Baidu Maps Geocoding API or other China-specific mapping services, as they generally offer higher accuracy for Chinese addresses compared to Google Maps API.
  • Access Government Data: Employ a Chinese proxy server or VPN to potentially overcome IP restrictions and access official government databases or environmental disclosure platforms for more comprehensive and verified facility lists.
  • Acquire Commercial Datasets: Research and potentially purchase commercially available datasets focused on the Chinese chemical industry (identified options were under $100), which may offer more detailed and curated information than publicly available sources.
